# 🔷 1) BAS / CA / CPA RISK BRIEF

**Independent Data Access, Record Retention & Continuity in Cloud Accounting (Xero)**

---

## Executive Summary

Cloud accounting platforms such as Xero provide operational convenience but operate under a **shared responsibility model**. The platform maintains availability and infrastructure, however **responsibility for maintaining access to records and retaining supporting evidence remains with the user (and their advisor)**.

For BAS agents, accountants, and tax practitioners, this is not merely an operational issue. It directly touches:
- **Record retention obligations**
- **Evidentiary support for BAS/GST reporting**
- **Client file documentation requirements**
- **Professional duty-of-care and defensibility**

Control‑C introduces an **independent, read-only copy of accounting records outside Xero**, helping practices and clients maintain continuity, audit readiness, and evidentiary integrity.

---

## 1. The Core Issue: “Live System ≠ Record Archive”

Xero functions as a **live ledger**, not a formal archival system.

Implications:
- Records can change over time
- Access depends on subscription and platform availability
- There is no guaranteed point-in-time historical preservation exposed to users

This creates risk when:
- Evidence is required retrospectively
- Access is disrupted (technical, commercial, or operational)
- Data is changed, deleted, or disputed

---

## 2. Professional & Regulatory Considerations

### BAS Agent / Tax Practitioner Responsibilities

Practitioners are expected to maintain:
- Records of **services provided**
- Information **relied upon**
- Advice **given and received**

These must be:
- **Retained appropriately**
- **Available upon request**
- **Defensible in audit or review scenarios**

### ATO / IRD Record-Keeping Expectations

For GST/BAS and tax reporting:
- Supporting documents must substantiate reported amounts
- Records must be **accurate, complete, and retrievable**

⚠️ A live system alone does not guarantee:
- historical integrity
- access at the time of need
- independence of evidence

---

## 3. Xero-Specific Risk Factors

Key structural realities:
- Xero’s Terms of Use place responsibility on the user to maintain copies of their data
- Xero disclaims liability for data loss
- Xero is not designed to provide full system restore or rollback capabilities
- Access to data depends on:&#x20;
  - subscription status
  - platform availability
  - account control (subscriber ownership)

---

## 4. The Practical Risk Scenarios

BAS agents and firms are exposed when:
- A client is audited and historic records are incomplete or unavailable
- A dispute arises and evidence has changed
- The client loses access (billing, ownership, or platform issue)
- Xero is unavailable during reporting deadlines
- Records are accidentally altered or deleted

---
